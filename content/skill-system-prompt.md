## The Mercury Deal

Christian's clearest example of selling financial technology to a startup team, not just into it. Mercury is a neobank built on Synapse's Banking-as-a-Service infrastructure. Christian sold Mercury on migrating their entire card program off Jack Henry, their legacy core system, onto Synapse's Mastercard stack.

**The problem:** Jack Henry had stand-in card limits — when its systems went down, transactions still processed past customers' approved limits. That meant fraud exposure and a capped product: no custom issuing limits, no richer controls.

**The approach:** Christian built the case at the top, with CEO Immad Akhund, which meant understanding Mercury's roadmap well enough to show where the migration fit — not just what it fixed today. Three-part argument: more interchange revenue, less fraud loss, and issuing controls Jack Henry couldn't support. He also worked out how Mercury would frame the reissuance to their own customers, turning a migration into a product story instead of a disruption.

**Why it worked:** Reissuing cards to hundreds of thousands of cardholders is a massive lift, and every Mercury stakeholder saw it as a pain point at first. Christian multithreaded engineering, compliance, and finance — each with different objections — but the deal moved because the case was airtight at the CEO level first. Seven-figure deal, hundreds of thousands of cardholders touched.

**Why it matters for Natural:** Mercury didn't ask for the migration — Christian identified it as the next step in their roadmap and built the case for it. That's the instinct a Founding GTM hire needs with startups building on agents: know the roadmap well enough to show them where infrastructure fits before they've named the need.

The GTM hires who struggle are the ones who sell the new thing without accounting for the old constraints. Christian's approach, in order:

Most GTM hires at agent-native startups have only sold the new part. Christian has spent four years selling into the old part too — that's the gap he closes.
